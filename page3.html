Working

In recent times, robots have become a significant component in both industrial and domestic settings. These robots can be manually or automatically controlled, and can perform tasks such as serving, file transfers, and security functions. This study focuses on the development of a waiter robot, which has been proposed as a potential solution to restaurant automation. Across the globe, robotics technology is rapidly replacing manual labor. Traditional cafes, restaurants, and hotels often face problems such as congestion during peak hours, waiter unavailability, and manual order processing. However, these shortcomings can be resolved by implementing a restaurant automation system that utilizes "Smart Serving Robots" for food and beverage orders. The orders are transmitted wirelessly to the kitchen via a menu bar. The robot, which is based on the Arduino, Keypad, and Bluetooth module, facilitates communication between the kitchen and reception. It then transports the food to the customers, with an in-built Wi-Fi camera providing two-way audio communication. The camera can be remotely controlled from anywhere in the world and files can also be transferred from one location to another.

Introduction to Smart Serving Robot using Node MCU

The Smart Serving Robot is a unique creation that is designed to provide efficient and convenient service to customers in a variety of settings. This robot is a semi-automated device that can be controlled through an app that is made using MIT App Inventor. The robot is driven by a NodeMCU unit that is connected to a Wi-Fi module. The head of the robot is equipped with a two-way communication webcam, which provides audio and video communication between the robot and the users.

Circuit description


The Smart Serving Robot is driven by a NodeMCU unit that is connected to a Wi-Fi module. The NodeMCU unit is an open-source development board that is based on the ESP8266 Wi-Fi module. It is equipped with a USB port, which is used to power the board and upload code to it. The Wi-Fi module is used to establish a wireless connection between the robot and the users. The robot's head is equipped with a two-way communication webcam, which provides audio and video communication between the robot and the users. The camera is connected to the NodeMCU unit, which processes the audio and video signals and sends them to the users through the app. The app is made using MIT App Inventor, which is a visual programming environment that allows users to create apps for Android devices. The app is used to control the movement of the robot and to communicate with the users through the audio and video signals provided by the webcam.

Pro's

Some of the pros include its ability to provide efficient and convenient service to customers, reducing the need for human intervention, and improving the overall customer experience. It can also be customized to suit different settings, making it suitable for use in a variety of industries

Con's

the high cost of development and maintenance, the need for technical expertise to operate and maintain the robot, and concerns about privacy and security.
