Working

A sensor tap, also known as a touchless or automatic faucet, is a plumbing fixture that operates without physical contact. It uses infrared or other sensors to detect hand presence, triggering a solenoid valve to allow water flow. Sensor taps are popular in public restrooms and commercial kitchens for their hygiene and water conservation benefits. They come in various styles and finishes and offer convenience, reduced water usage, and improved hygiene.

Introduction to Sensor Tap

Once the sensor tap is powered on, it is primed to detect any obstruction or object, such as a hand placed in front of it. When this occurs, the solenoid valve is triggered, allowing water to flow from the outlet to the inlet of the tap. After five seconds, the water flow is halted, but if the object remains in place, it will restart after a two-second delay as the hand is detected once again.

Circuit description


To begin with, the ultrasonic sensor's VCC and GND must be connected to the corresponding pins on the Arduino, or a digital pin can be configured as high and low to supply 5 volts to the sensor. The Trig and Echo pins must then be linked to digital pins, and the code must be written to transmit signals from Trig and receive them from Echo every millisecond. This forms the crux of the circuit's operation.

In the second stage, a 12-volt solenoid must be controlled in response to signals transmitted from the Arduino. To accomplish this, a circuit must be established that can operate with a 5-volt input signal and provide a 12-volt output to the solenoid. To achieve this, I have developed a circuit that employs a 5-volt relay connected to an NPN transistor. When an input signal is received from the Arduino on the transistor's P pin, it activates the relay and completes the circuit, causing the solenoid to be switched on. In this manner, the entire circuit functions.

Pro's

  1. Offer more significant water savings, Wastes less water : Conservation Of Water up to 70%.

  2. Working both indoor and outdoor purpose example it can work with 100% efficiency not only in room conditions but also in sunlight conditions.

Con's

  1. Power supply is mandatory or compulsory i.e standby power supply is required like an inverter or ups

  2. When the sensor tap is in use more than 6-8 hours (average time) then we can observe little heating due to solenoid coil.
